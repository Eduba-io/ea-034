01 / The Eduba frame

Most agentic rollouts over-index on the model and under-index on the layer above it.

We work on a simple idea. About 60% of what an analyst workflow needs is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a genuine AI problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is shipped, and documenting the sort so a federal reviewer can follow it.

Applied to the Babel Street stack, the map resolves quickly. Entity resolution and multilingual analytics (Rosette heritage) are a code-and-model layer. Source ingestion across hundreds of PAI and CAI feeds is a database-and-pipeline layer. Analyst tradecraft rules (what to elevate, when to flag, how to cite) are rule-based logic. The genuinely agentic surface is narrower than the announcement reads and more defensible when the boundary is drawn on purpose.

The provenance evidence a federal customer needs during ATO review is produced by that boundary being drawn cleanly, not by a post-hoc annotation pass over model outputs. That is the sort of work we would propose to do with the product and AI team.

02 / The relevant case study
VigilOre Multi-agent compliance Document-heavy workflow Tiered retainer plus revenue share

A multi-agent platform for DRC mining compliance with the same structural problem as an OSINT investigative workflow: high-stakes output, long source chains, regulator-grade citation requirements, and a human in the final loop. We drew the boundary between deterministic documentation and agentic reasoning at the start of the build. Compliance work that had consumed more than 160 hours per cycle collapsed to under five minutes with provenance intact.
